**CYBERBULLYING** 



Cyberbullying refers to online harassment that takes place on social media sites, messaging apps or other digital platforms . It involves sending abusive messages, spreading rumors, sharing private information, or posting embarrassing content to harm someone emotionally or socially.



I remember an incident which happened with a first-year college student who joined a new college and  used social media to connect with classmates. A group of students started making fun of her posts and appearance by commenting and messaging privately. Gradually, they create a fake account using her name and upload edited images to mock her publicly. The situation worsens when these posts are shared widely leading to humiliation and isolation.



Cyberbullying typically happens in steps: first, the target is identified; then harassment begins through messages or comments; next, the abuse escalates with fake profiles or public posts; and finally, it spreads to a larger audience, creating a huge impact.



Students and teenagers are the most common targets because they are active social media users  and often unaware of privacy settings and reporting options. The consequences includes anxiety, depression, loss of self-confidence, academic downfall and self-harm.



This case highlights the importance of  awareness, and timely reporting of such incidents to prevent long-term psychological harm.
